Projected Income Statement

Sales
Our sales for 2013 were based upon our sales in 2012. In 2012, 20% 0f our sales was from the gain (loss) on sale of land and equipment. Since we are not planning selling any land in 2013 we subtracted the 20% from the sales of 2012. However we expect a 14% growth in sales as a result of our newly hired regional manager. Therefore, we are expecting our 2013 sales to be 2% less than the sales of 2012. The 2% decrease in sales of 2012 gives projected sales of $2,838,000 for 2013. To that projected sales we added the deferred revenue from 2012 ($412,000) and come up with the final projected sales of $3,250,000 for 2013.
Cost of Goods Sold
The cost of goods sold was taken as a percentage of the sales for each year. All of the percentages were within a 5% range. The average of these percentages is 35.5%. 35.5% of the sales give the cost of goods sold for 2013.
Wage Expense
The cost of goods percentage of sales for 2011 and 2012 were within 2% of each other, allowing the assumption that 2013’s wage expense will be close to the same percentage of sales as the previous years. The average cost of goods sold percentage of sales for 2011, and 2012 was calculated to be 21.5%. 21.5% of the sales for 2013 ($ 2,838,000; sales excluding deferred income from 2012) was calculated to be $610,170. Then the wages payable of $170,000 from 2012 was added to give the projected wage expense of $780,170. 2013’s wage expense is quite a bit higher than the wage expense of other years. This is justifiable because $170,000 of it is actually payable from 2012.
Gross Profit
The cost of goods sold subtracted from the sales. The gross income is higher than previous years than the previous years because of the boost in sales the deferred revenue from 2012. The gross profit was so high because the deferred revenue that boosted the sales had no corresponding cost of goods sold because that was recorded in 2012.
Advertising Expense
The advertising expense for 2012 was $2727 per month. We will be keeping the same advertising for all of 2013, making the advertising expense for 12 months $32,727.
Utilities Expense
The utilities expense is a fixed yearly expense and will not differ in 2013.
Office Supplies Expense
The office supplies expense is a fixed yearly rate and will not differ in 2013.
Fuel Expense
The fuel expense per month in 2012 was $900. We plan on using the usage of fuel to be the same throughout 2013, giving an expense of $10,800 for 12 months.
Insurance Expense
The insurance expense per month in 2012 for a 2 year insurance policy is $7,500. The policy will cover the entirety of 2013, giving an insurance expense of $90,000 for the year.
Rent Expense
The monthly rent expense from 2012 was $2,500, making the rent expense for the entirety of 2013, $30,000.
Amortization Expense
The yearly straight-line amortization of a patent over10 years gives an expense of $4000 per year.
Bad Debt Expense
The sales divided by the average accounts receivable turnover for 2011 an 2012, which is 4.2 gives the projected accounts receivable of $ 675,714 for 2013. Since the inception of our company we have been able to collect 85% of our ending accounts receivable balance, making bad debt expense 15% of the accounts receivable.
Depreciation Expense
The sum of an ongoing straight-line depreciation ($500,000), straight-line office equipment depreciation ($5,000) and year 2 of double declining depreciation of a truck ($44063) gives a depreciation expense of $549,063.
Operating Income
Operating expenses subtracted from the gross profit. The operating income is higher than previous years because the sales of 2013 is higher than other years and the cost of goods sold is lower than others years due to the $412,000 of deferred revenue from 2012. None of the previous years had any deferred revenue from earlier years that raised their sales like is done in 2013.
Interest Income
3% (the interest rate) of the average of ending cash balances of 2012/2011 ($2,736,750), which gives the interest income of $82,103.
Interest Expense
The sum of the interest expense for the long term note payable ($5,000) and the interest payable for the short term note payable ($7,500), giving $12,500.
Bond Interest Expense
3% of the premium on bonds payable ($4,325) subtracted from bond price ($1,045,000) giving a bond interest expense of $31,220.

Net Income
